MY FAVORITE TRAVEL CITIES

San Francisco

I have traveled to this city MANYtimes. The city by the bay never disappoints - from the beautiful golden gate bridge, delicious bowl of clam chowder at Fisherman's wharf, to a great ride on a famous cable car. When traveling here, make sure to pack clothes for all types of weather; you can be sweating in January and freezing in July - as Mark Twain famously said "The coldest winter I ever saw was the summer I spent in San Francisco."

New York City

"The City that Never Sleeps" was a nickname coined by Frank Sinatra about New York. SO true - the hustle and bustle and constant go go of this city makes for an exciting trip. When you are here, visit the Statue of Liberty, ride to the top of the Empire State Building, Rockfeller Center is always fun, and never leave without seeing a Broadway show. We also walked across the Brookly Bridge and had a slice from the famous pizzera, Grimaldi's in DUMBO. Also, attending a game in Yankee Stadium was another highlight of our trip.

Pittsburgh

Pittsburgh?!! The Steel City or our nickname, FOOTBALL HEAVEN!It's true, our favorite thing to do in the 'burgh is to attend a football game at Heinz Field. We also love driving by the boyhood home of my husband, John and grabbing a delicious AUTHENTIC bagel from a bagel shop in Squirrel Hill. ( Saint Louis does not know what a bagel is!) Also a stop at Primanti Brothers for a famous cheesesteak sub. Less you think we only eat in Pittsburgh, a visit to Kennywood Theme Park, touring the Andy Warhol museum, or drive out to see Frank Lloyd Wright's famous Falling Waters are some activities to add to the agenda.
